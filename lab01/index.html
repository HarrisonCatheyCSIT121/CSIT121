Baby Lima Beans

Recipe by Ronald Cathey (father of author)

This recipe was created by my Dad. The original recipe came from the back of a packet of lima beans, but has been modified with few different ingredients and a different process. This is a hearty dish that is great for cold days, and should be served over rice. This is my favorite dish because it is easy to make, cheap, healthy, and delicious.

Ingredients

  • 2 Yellow Onions
  • 2 Greeen Peppers
  • 2 Stalks of Celery
  • 5-6 Toes of Garlic
  • 1 Pound of Andouille Sausage
  • 1 Pound of Bacon
  • 1 Packet of Baby Lima Beans
  • 8 Cups Vegetable Stock

Recipe Instructions

  1. Sort and rinse the beans.
  2. Add the beans to a large pot and add vegetable stock.
  3. Bring the beans to a rolling boil for at least 10 minutes.
  4. Wash and dice the onions, green peppers, and celery. Dice can be coarse, as it will all be cooked down. Put all the diced veggies aside in a container.
  5. Finely dice the garlic and put it in a separate container.
  6. Cut the andouille sausage into roughly 1/2" coins. Cut the bacon to be about the same thickness.
  7. Add some oil to a pan and put it over high heat.
  8. Once the oil is hot, add the meat. Brown each side for about a minute then remove from the pan.
  9. Add the vegetables to the pan. Cook until the onions are glassy, while stirring often.
  10. Add garlic to the pan.
  11. When garlic is fragrant, turn off the heat and add vegetables to the pot with the beans.
  12. Add the meat to the pot with the veggies and beans.
  13. Bring pot up to a boil again, then lower heat until liquid is at a low simmer.
  14. Simmer for 1 hour, or until beans are tender.
  15. Skim oil from surface, then season to taste.
  16. Turn off heat, serve over rice, and enjoy!
